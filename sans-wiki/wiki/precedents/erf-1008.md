# ERF 1008 — Stair Width Non-Compliant for H4 Occupancy

## Property Details
- **ERF Size:** 650m²
- **Existing improvements:** Single storey dwelling (approved 2011)
- **Proposed works:** Convert loft above garage to habitable room — new internal stair required

## Departments Reviewed
| Dept | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| BC | ❌ REJECTED | Stair width non-compliant for H4 occupancy |

## Key Decision Points

### Occupancy Classification (Clause B1, SANS 10400)
- Proposed loft room: bedroom / sleeping quarters
- When used for sleeping, this triggers H4 (Institutional — Residential) classification
- H4 occupancy has stricter means of egress requirements than H3 (Dwelling house)

### Stair Width Requirement (Clause D1, SANS 10400)
- H3 (Dwelling house): minimum clear stair width 850mm
- H4 (Institutional — Residential): minimum clear stair width **1,100mm**
- Proposed stair: 900mm clear width
- **Non-compliance: 200mm shortfall** — 18% below minimum

### Other Issues
1. **Headroom:** Proposed stair headroom 2.0m at pitch point — H4 requires 2.1m minimum
2. **Handrail:** Proposed one-sided handrail — H4 requires both sides for stair width >900mm
3. **Landings:** Landing at top: 900mm × 900mm — H4 requires 1,100mm × 1,100mm minimum

### Why This Can't Be Fixed With Conditions
- The 900mm stair is structurally integrated into the loft conversion design
- Widening to 1,100mm would require reducing loft room width from 3.6m to 3.0m — commercially unviable
- The stair configuration is a fundamental design choice that cannot be retrofitted
- This is not a case where conditions can resolve the issue — redesign required

## Decision
**REJECTED** — stair width, headroom, handrail, and landing non-compliant with SANS 10400-D1 for H4 occupancy.

Advice to applicant: Redesign loft conversion as H3 (non-sleeping) use — or reconsider the entire addition approach.

## Lessons Learned
- Sleep accommodation in loft triggers H4, not H3 — check intended use carefully
- H4 has significantly stricter means of egress requirements
- Internal stairs for lofts need careful attention to occupancy classification
- Early engagement with building control on occupancy classification would have saved significant cost
