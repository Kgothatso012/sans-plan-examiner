## Key Decision Points

### Energy Performance (XA1, SANS 10400 / National Building Regulations)
- SANS 10400-XA (2011 as amended) requires thermal performance compliance
- All new work must demonstrate:
  - Wall thermal insulation: R-value ≥ 1.0 m²K/W
  - Ceiling/roof insulation: R-value ≥ 2.0 m²K/W
  - glazing: Maximum solar heat gain coefficient (SHGC) 0.48
  - Overall building thermal performance must meet energy efficiency targets

### Initial Submission
- Application submitted without XA1 energy calculations
- Submitted plans showed standard brick walls, no insulation detail
- Thermal compliance not addressed at all

### First Review: XA1 Documents Requested
- Applicant engaged energy consultant
- Consultant submitted thermal modeling showing:
  - Walls: cavity brick with 50mm insulation = R 1.3 m²K/W ✅
  - Ceiling: 135mm roof insulation = R 2.8 m²K/W ✅
  - Glazing: aluminum frame with clear glazing SHGC 0.48 ✅
  - Overall: 62 kWh/m²/year — **within SANS 10400-XA limits** ✅

### Coverage (Clause H2)
- Existing: 38%
- Addition: +7.2%
- **Total: 45.2%** ✅ — within 60%

### Setbacks (Tshwane Scheme Clause 20)
- Rear setback: 3.0m ✅
- Side setback: 1.5m ✅
- First floor addition within envelope ✅

## Decision
**CONDITIONAL APPROVAL** — granted on provision of:
1. Amended drawings showing XA1 insulation specifications (cavity insulation, ceiling insulation R-values)
2. Energy consultant certificate of compliance (SANS 10400-XA)
3. Glazing schedule showing SHGC ≤ 0.48

## Lessons Learned
- XA1 is frequently overlooked by residential architects — check early
- Energy consultant engagement should happen before first submission
- XA1 doesn't have to be complex — standard insulation spec often suffices
- SANS 10400-XA has been mandatory since 2011 — no grandfathering for new work
- Penalty for non-compliance is refusal, not a fine — get it right first time
