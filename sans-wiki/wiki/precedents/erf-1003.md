# ERF 1003 — Major Coverage Exceedance — Rejected

## Property Details
- **ERF Size:** 495m² (very small erf)
- **Existing improvements:** Single dwelling, carport
- **Proposed works:** 60m² double-storey addition (kitchen + 2 bedrooms above)

## Departments Reviewed
| Dept | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| BC | ❌ REJECTED | Non-compliant with SANS 10400-H2 |
| RSP | ❌ REJECTED | Coverage exceedance not motivatable |

## Key Decision Points

### Coverage (Clause H2, SANS 10400)
This application was fundamentally non-compliant. Calculations:

- Existing footprint: 285m² (58%)
- Proposed addition: 60m²
- **Total coverage: 345m² = 69.7%** ❌
- R1 limit: 60% maximum
- **Exceedance: 9.7 percentage points**

### Why Rejected
Clause H2 of SANS 10400 and Tshwane Scheme Clause 20 are unambiguous:
- 60% is the hard maximum for R1
- The erf is only 495m² — adding 60m² on this size erf made compliance mathematically impossible
- No relaxation route available for this level of exceedance
- Even if addition reduced to minimum useful size (~20m²), coverage would still be ~61.6%

### Refusal Reasons
1. Site coverage 69.7% — exceeds 60% maximum by 9.7 percentage points
2. No technical justification or site constraints presented
3. Relaxation not available for this magnitude of exceedance

## Decision
**REJECTED**

## Advice Given to Applicant
- Reduce addition to ~10m² to achieve 59.5% — still within limit
- Or apply for rezoning to allow higher coverage (complex, long process)
- Or purchase adjacent erf to increase site area

## Lessons Learned
- On small erfs (<600m²), even modest additions can cause coverage problems
- Pre-submission advice should flag this before owner spends on architects
- 60% is a hard cap — no discretionary relief available
