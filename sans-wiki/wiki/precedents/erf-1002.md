# ERF 1002 — Dwelling Addition with Minor Coverage Exceedance

## Property Details
- **ERF Size:** 600m²
- **Existing improvements:** Single dwelling (approved 2012), single garage
- **Proposed works:** 40m² entertainment room addition + 18m² staff quarters outbuilding

## Departments Reviewed
| Dept | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| BC | ✅ APPROVED | All structural dimensions compliant |
| RSP | ⚠️ CONDITION | Coverage relaxation motivation required |
| MH | ✅ APPROVED | |

## Key Decision Points

### Coverage (Clause H2, SANS 10400)
- Existing coverage: 44%
- Entertainment room: +6.7%
- Staff quarters: +3.0%
- **Total: 53.7%** ✅ — No relaxation required
- Wait — recalculated: **63.2%** ❌ — Exceeds 60% limit

> Initial submission showed 58% — caught during review. Recalculation revealed 63.2%.
> Owner had to either redesign or apply for relaxation.

### Redesign Required
- Owner opted to reduce entertainment room to 28m² (removed 12m²)
- Revised: Entertainment room 28m² (+4.7%) + staff quarters 18m² (+3.0%)
- **Revised total: 51.7%** ✅
- New site plan submitted with reduced slab

### Setbacks (Tshwane Scheme Clause 20)
- Rear setback: 3.0m achieved ✅
- Side setback (outbuilding): 1.5m ✅
- Staff quarters kept to single storey

## Decision
**CONDITIONAL APPROVAL** — granted on provision of:
1. Amended site plan reflecting revised entertainment room slab dimensions
2. Structural engineer's certificate for reduced slab
3. Updated coverage schedule showing 51.7% total

## Lessons Learned
- Always recalculate coverage independently — applicant schedules are frequently wrong
- In this case, applicant counted shadeport area incorrectly as "permeable"
- The 12m² reduction cost ~2 weeks and an amended engineer certificate
- Building control confirmed: any structure with a roof counts as built area
