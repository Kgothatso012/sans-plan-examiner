# ERF 1006 — Office to Medical Practice Change of Use

## Property Details
- **ERF Size:** 1,250m²
- **Existing improvements:** Two-storey office building (approved 2005), 8 parking bays
- **Proposed works:** Change of use from general office to medical practice (doctor's rooms)

## Departments Reviewed
| Dept | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| BC | ✅ APPROVED | Compliant with SANS 10400-B2 fire resistance |
| RSP | ✅ APPROVED | Parking calculation revised — 6 bays required, 8 existing |
| EH | ✅ APPROVED | Medical waste facilities must be registered separately |

## Key Decision Points

### Change of Use vs New Building
- Existing building structure remains unchanged — no additions
- Change of use triggers different occupancy and facility requirements
- Medical practice classified H4 (Institutional — Ambulatory Care) under SANS 10400-B1
- Existing building was classified B3 (Office) — different requirements

### Fire Resistance (Clause B2, SANS 10400)
- Medical practice (H4) requires 120 min fire resistance for structural elements
- Existing building: 60 min rated — **NOT COMPLIANT**
- Applicant submitted amended fire engineering certificate
- Existing structure to be upgraded with intumescent paint to 120 min
- **Condition: fire upgrade must be completed before occupancy**

### Parking (Clause F1, Tshwane Scheme)
- Medical practice: 1 bay per 30m² consulting area + 1 per practitioner
- Consulting rooms: 85m² → 3 bays
- 2 practitioners → 2 additional bays
- **Total required: 5 bays**
- Existing: 8 bays ✅ — surplus, no relaxation needed

### Accessibility (SANS 10400-D)
- Ramp required at entrance (existing step of 3 steps = 540mm)
- Existing ramp provided: 1:12 gradient — **within SANS 10400-D limits** ✅
- Accessible toilet required — existing has one ✅

### Ventilation (Clause G2, SANS 10400)
- Mechanical ventilation required for procedure rooms
- HVAC engineer certification submitted

## Decision
**APPROVED** — granted on provision of:
1. Fire upgrade to 120 min structural rating (intumescent paint application certificate)
2. HVAC engineer certificate for procedure room ventilation
3. EH registration for medical waste storage

## Lessons Learned
- Change of use applications are more complex than additions — always check the new occupancy class requirements
- Fire resistance requirements can increase significantly with change of use
- Medical practices require EH involvement — budget for that timeline
- Parking surplus from office use often covers medical practice requirements
